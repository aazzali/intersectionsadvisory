What we offer

Technical excellence at every intersection of child protection and humanitarian action.

Four service areas, each drawing on field experience across West and Central Africa, the Middle East, and global programme contexts.

Services

01

Technical Advisory

Strategic and operational guidance on child protection in migration and displacement contexts. We work with organisations to strengthen CP case management systems, develop quality standards, improve inter-agency coordination, and design nexus programming that functions across humanitarian and development modalities.

Particular depth in: UASC, stateless children, trafficking survivors, asylum seekers, and children in mixed migration flows across Francophone West and Central Africa and the Middle East. Available in English and French across the full mandate cycle.

02

Programme Architecture

Design of the frameworks, standards, and systems that translate child protection intent into operationally coherent responses — built for field reality, not only for compliance.

We develop Quality Assessment Frameworks, indicator menus, capacity building toolkits, and monitoring systems that practitioners can actually use. Our most recent global mandate produced SCI's CP Case Management guidance for migration contexts — a three-part package now being rolled out across SCI's global portfolio.

We bring the same rigour to smaller-scale assignments: a single country programme, a regional coordination mechanism, a partner capacity assessment.

03

Ethics in Practice

Humanitarian action in displacement contexts raises ethical questions that technical frameworks alone cannot answer. Who gets counted? Whose protection is prioritised when resources are scarce? What does accountability to affected children actually look like when those children cannot speak for themselves?

We work with organisations navigating safeguarding, do-no-harm programming, PSEA, accountability to affected populations, and the particular moral complexity of working with children in fragile, conflict-affected, and migration settings. This is not compliance training — it is structured engagement with the hard questions that determine programme quality.

04

Mentoring

Professional mentoring for child protection and migration practitioners at inflection points — moving into technical advisory roles, leading regional portfolios for the first time, navigating the transition from field to headquarters, or building a career at the intersection of humanitarian and development work.

Mentoring engagements are individually tailored. They draw on twenty years of experience across three regions — including lessons from what went wrong, not only what worked.
