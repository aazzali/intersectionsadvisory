About

Amanda Azzali

Amanda holds a degree in International Relations and Diplomatic Sciences from the University of Trieste and an Advanced Specialization in International Cooperation from ENFAP Emilia Romagna. Over twenty years, she has dedicated her work to the protection of the most marginalised and vulnerable children across three continents — West and Central Africa, the Middle East, and globally. She would do it all over again.

Her particular combination is specific and rare: child protection technical standard-setting, migration programming, nexus programme architecture, and regional capacity building, operating across humanitarian and development modalities in both English and French.

She builds the frameworks, regional architectures, and capacity building systems that translate protection intent into consistent, high-quality responses for children in displacement and migration settings.
